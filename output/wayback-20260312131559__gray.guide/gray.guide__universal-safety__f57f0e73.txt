Universal Safety Principles: Overview & General Injection Safety
This section provides core safety guidance for
all
GLP-1 RA users, no matter how the medication is sourced or administered (except oral tablets). Master these basics before attempting any injection.
General Injection Safety Concepts
​
Although the practical steps vary by path, three foundational rules apply to every injection and help prevent infection or injury:
Cleanliness (Aseptic Technique):
Start by washing your hands thoroughly with soap and water. Use a brand-new sterile needle and syringe for each shot. Wipe both the vial stopper and your skin with separate alcohol swabs and let each surface air-dry before puncturing or injecting.
Injection-Site Rotation:
Do not inject into the exact same spot repeatedly. Rotating between areas (abdomen, thighs, upper arms) prevents lumps such as lipohypertrophy and promotes reliable absorption.
Safe Sharps Disposal:
Place every used needle, syringe, or single-use pen directly into an approved puncture-resistant sharps container. Never toss loose sharps into household trash or recycling. (See the
Safe Disposal Guide
for details.)
Absolute Rule: No Sharing Devices / Needles
​
danger
Never share injection pens, vials, syringes, or needles - even if you swap the needle.
Sharing any of this equipment can transmit blood-borne infections such as HIV and hepatitis B or C. The rule applies to supplies used in Paths A, B, and C. Each person must have their own dedicated set.
