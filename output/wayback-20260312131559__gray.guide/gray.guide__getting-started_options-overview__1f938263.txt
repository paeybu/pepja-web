Understanding Your Options
This section outlines the three main approaches discussed. Understanding the characteristics, requirements, and risks of each path is essential before you proceed. Decide which route matches your budget, skill level, and risk tolerance.

Option A: (Pen, Pre-filled Syringe, Tablet)
​
This option includes GLP-1 products sold as ready-to-use pens, single-dose syringes, or tablets. They need almost no preparation and are often imported through gray-market channels that bypass local prescription requirements.

Who is this for?
People who want the simplest setup with almost no preparation
Users willing to handle the uncertainties of gray-market shipping
Anyone who prefers to avoid mixing or measuring doses
Option B: (Mounjaro® Tirzepatide Vial)
​
Mounjaro® (tirzepatide) single-dose vial – Eli Lilly India
Indian-market Mounjaro vials are manufactured by Eli Lilly and use the same serialised packaging as US products. You can confirm authenticity with
scan.lilly.com
. Thanks to purchasing-power parity, the price per vial is lower in India, making this the safest and most cost-effective gray-market choice.
Vials are available only in 2.5 mg and 5 mg strengths. If you need a larger dose such as 10 mg, buy two 5 mg vials and inject both. Each vial is single use, so no dose measurement is required – draw the entire contents into a syringe and inject once.
Who is this for?
People who want authentic branded medication from the gray market
Those seeking lower prices than domestic pharmacies
Users willing to manage international shipping and inject a single-dose vial
Option C: The DIY Powder (Lyophilized Peptides)
​
Lyophilized peptide powder labelled "Research Use Only"
This route buys semaglutide or tirzepatide as freeze-dried powder from online "research chemical" vendors. Labels state
"for research use only"
and
not for human use
.
You must master several technical steps:
Reconstitution:
Mix the powder with sterile bacteriostatic water without contaminating it.
Dosage Calculation:
Work out the exact volume to inject. A small math error can cause severe over- or under-dosing.
Drawing and Injecting:
Pull the measured dose into an insulin syringe and give the shot subcutaneously.
Per-dose cost is lowest, but hazards are highest – unknown purity, sterility, and potency, plus a much larger chance of user error. Independent lab testing is expensive and rarely performed.
Who is this for?
People chasing the lowest possible cost and willing to shoulder serious risk
Users prepared to research, calculate, and follow strict sterile technique
Those comfortable sourcing supplies, mixing solutions, and performing their own injections
This path is dangerous
because the product is unverified and the procedure is complex
