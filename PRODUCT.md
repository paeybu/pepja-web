# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Thai adults who have already decided to research peptides — for weight loss (GLP-1s like semaglutide and tirzepatide), recovery (BPC-157), longevity, growth hormone, cosmetic. They've found the English-language community (Reddit r/Peptides, gray.guide, Stairway to Gray, various forums) and want that same quality of information in Thai. Most read on mobile, often at night. They're not shopping for a clinic consult and they're not shopping for a pharmacy storefront. They want mechanism, dosing, real trade-offs, and a clear picture of where in Thailand they can actually source what they need, without being talked down to or sold at.

## Product Purpose

Pepja is the Thai-language counterpart to gray.guide and Stairway to Gray: a community-quality, harm-reduction reference for peptide use, written in Thai, for adults who can read and decide for themselves. It exists because the information already exists in English; pepja translates, reorganizes, and adapts it so a Thai reader is not held hostage by language. Success is when a Thai reader leaves a page with the same depth they would have gotten from the English community, in their own language, without being upsold to a clinic or scared off by paternalism.

## Brand Personality

Direct. Honest. Respectful.

Speaks to the reader as an adult who has already chosen to research the topic. No hedging, no hype, no fear. The voice is closer to a smart friend who did the homework and is sharing notes than to a doctor, a pharmacist, or a marketer. Editorial confidence without commercial polish. The visual register that supports this is warm consumer-health, anchored to gray.guide and Stairway to Gray, not Thai clinic websites or wellness DTC brands.

## Anti-references

What pepja must never look like:

- **Hype supplement and bro-science sites.** No "GAIN MUSCLE NOW" claims, no transformation photos, no neon urgency banners, no "limited time" pressure, no testosterone-bro voice.
- **Fearmongering health blogs.** No red warning blocks on every page, no paternalistic "consult a doctor before" sprinkled into every paragraph, no AdSense-soup layout, no ominous tone designed to drive clicks.
- **Generic AI-template SaaS.** No hero-metric block (big number, small label), no three-up identical icon cards, no gradient-on-dark "Get started free" pattern, no "Built with" badges. The site must be unmistakable, not interchangeable with any other modern landing page.

## Design Principles

1. **Harm reduction over gatekeeping.** Design choices reduce friction to good information; they never create artificial barriers. No 18+ interstitials, no signup walls, no required disclaimers blocking content.
2. **Information first, identity through it.** Dose tables, callouts, trade-off notes, and sourcing context are the product. Visual identity amplifies legibility, it does not compete with it.
3. **Trade-offs are visible, not hidden.** Risks and costs (clinic markup, research-grade purity, side effects, legal status) get the same visual weight as benefits. No buried disclaimers, no fine-print risk text.
4. **Mobile is the baseline, not the afterthought.** Layouts, line lengths, tap targets, and reading rhythm are designed for one-thumb phone reading. Desktop is the bonus.
5. **Color is wayfinding, not decoration.** Each peptide category (GLP-1, healing, growth, longevity, cosmetic) and guide type (how-to, concept, safety, sourcing) owns a color, so a reader can orient by glance. Color carries meaning; it is never garnish.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **Mobile-first.** All layouts validated on phone widths before desktop. Tap targets at least 44 px. Body text at a comfortable mobile size without zooming.
- **Thai script as first-class.** Body type must be Thai-optimized (currently IBM Plex Sans Thai Looped). Latin-first fonts that fall back to ugly Thai rendering are not acceptable. Line height and letter spacing tuned for Thai, which sits taller than Latin.
- **Dark mode as first-class.** Already shipped. Dark is not an afterthought toggle; it gets equal craft, equal contrast, and an equally considered palette.
- **No formal WCAG mandate.** Readable contrast is a baseline of craft, but pepja does not currently claim a specific WCAG level. Don't sprinkle a11y bullets as cargo cult; commit to what is actually maintained.
